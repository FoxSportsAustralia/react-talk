A couple of Alternatives

Worthy mentions investigated
  • Jade
    • the templating language was first implemented (just before react) as the first step to cleaning up our templates.
    • The idea being we'd focus closer to the relevant parts of the dom we need to manipulate
    • Skip reprinting existing doms
    • Problems
      • New markup to learn (I still didn't get my hands too dirty with it)
      • Required us to calculate what was, and wasn't needed on the page
      • Was dreadfully convoluted when it came to updating existing dom when events details updated (location x/y update but not whom scored etc.)
  • Angular
    • AngularJS, google's "what HTML would have been"
    • Clean templates
    • Excelled in repeating items with ng-repeat
    • Directives great point for code reuse
    • Problems
      • Would have required a rewrite of nearly 100% (Their directives/modules/services/factories)
      • AT THE TIME - Still reprinted the dom. (pre v2.0)
      • Was personally concerned with imminent v2 launch, changed apis, community very aggressive
  • Backbone, ember, knockout, meteor
